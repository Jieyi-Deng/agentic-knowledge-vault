## Key Claims and Ideas

- Information structures must be understood as both technical and social artifacts.
- Data portability requires more than data movement; it requires preserved meaning, documentation, and usability.
- Metadata is central because it carries context, provenance, relationships, and interpretive support.
- Information access can be technically available but still practically inaccessible.
- User stories and information stories help align architecture with real goals.
- Knowledge graphs, ontologies, APIs, data dictionaries, and schemas are mechanisms for making information reusable across contexts.
- FAIR principles provide a useful rubric for evaluating portable information structures.
- Agentic AI systems introduce a new use case: the knowledge base must be usable by both humans and future agents.
